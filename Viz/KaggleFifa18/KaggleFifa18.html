FIFA 18 Player Data

About the Data


The FIFA 18 Player Data is available on Kaggle. It is a public data set and I downloaded it from here. It is the complete data set of players in FIFA 18. It contains 17995 rows and 185 columns. There are 253 records in the dataset with no "club" information. Since I am using the "club" column for the purpose of analysis, the records with missing information have been removed from the data set.


Key Takeaways from the Visualization

  • English Premier League is the highest paying soccer league in the world. It pays more than 57 times to a player with same overall skill in the Ukranian Super League and almost twice as more as German Bundesliga.
  • A player's value tends to increase with wage. Although there quite a few players who have high value but do not draw high wage.
  • Wage and Value are well-correlated and they both follow an almost normal distribution with a peak at around 30. However, the Wage peaks again at around 40 as the Value remains low. This can due to the fact that there are many soccer players, who used to very successful in their prime and still draw a lot of money due to their big names. Their value, however, do not increase.
  • Based on average overall skill, Spanish Primera Division seems to have the most talented players, followed by Ukranian Super League and German Bundesliga.
  • FC Barcelona, Real Madrid CF, FC Bayern Munich, Juventus, Manchester United and Chelsea are some of the highest paying soccer clubs.
